1. Project Overview

VisGait is an interactive D3.js dashboard for rehabilitation tracking and cohort analysis in the DH2321 project at KTH. The dashboard design is informed by recent smart knee-brace research (DOI: 10.1002/adhm.202202987), and the implementation is split into two targeted user experiences.

Patient View

The patient dashboard focuses on clear progress tracking over time. Patients can monitor weekly recovery, compare activity performance, and adjust goal-related controls without needing to interpret raw signals.

Researcher View

The researcher dashboard supports multivariate analysis across cohorts. Researchers can inspect distributions, correlations, and temporal trends to investigate gait anomalies and performance differences by category.

2. How To Use (Munzner's Framework)

Task abstraction and idiom choices follow Munzner's framework. See citation in References.

User I: Patients

The patient dashboard prioritizes clarity, motivation, and personalization.

  • Task 1 (Monitoring General Recovery)
    Analyze: Discover | Search: Explore | Query: Identify | Target: Trends
    Idiom: Weekly Progress Line Chart and Health Bar.
  • Task 2 (Identifying Suitable Activities)
    Analyze: Discover | Search: Explore | Query: Compare | Target: Extremes
    Idiom: Activities Overview (Radar) and Weekly Bar Charts.

User II: Researchers

The researcher dashboard supports deep cohort analysis and multidimensional correlation.

  • Task 1 (Visualizing statistical significance of gait metrics)
    Analyze: Discover | Search: Explore | Query: Summarize | Target: Distribution and Dependencies
    Idiom: Violin Plot and Scatter Plot Matrix.
  • Task 2 (Comparing specific attributes over time)
    Analyze: Discover | Search: Explore | Query: Compare | Target: Trends
    Idiom: Parallel Coordinate Plot and Line Plot with Standard Deviation.

Demo Video

Limitations

  • Synthetic Demonstration Data: Current longitudinal performance visuals include synthetic segments for demonstration.
  • Signal Processing Disclosure: Full transformation details from raw signal to all derived metrics are partially withheld while research is ongoing.
  • Web Responsiveness: Dense multivariate charts, especially scatter matrices and parallel coordinates, remain harder to read on small mobile screens.

4. Data Provenance & Abstraction

The source data originates from the smart knee braces and consists of raw timestamp and resistance signal for both the left and right knees. These raw signals are processed into derived gait metrics that are used throughout the dashboard.

The working dataset is stored in data/dashboard_data.csv. Variable descriptions for the derived metrics are documented in js/config.js under METRIC_INFO.

IPI_left_mean_sec

Left Inter-Peak Interval (Mean) - average time in seconds between consecutive left foot strikes. Lower values indicate faster stepping.

IPI_left_std_sec

Left Inter-Peak Interval (Std Dev) - variability of left foot strike timing in seconds. Higher values suggest less consistent gait.

IPI_right_mean_sec

Right Inter-Peak Interval (Mean) - average time in seconds between consecutive right foot strikes.

IPI_right_std_sec

Right Inter-Peak Interval (Std Dev) - variability of right foot strike timing in seconds.

cadence_total_steps_min

Total Cadence - combined steps per minute from both feet. A common clinical measure of walking speed.

gait_index_left_pct

Left Gait Index (%) - regularity score for the left leg's gait pattern. Higher values indicate a more consistent, periodic stride.

gait_index_right_pct

Right Gait Index (%) - regularity score for the right leg's gait pattern.

GSI_pct

Gait Symmetry Index (%) - measures overall symmetry between left and right gait. Lower values indicate more symmetric gait.

symmetry_ratio

Symmetry Ratio - ratio of left to right step timing. A value of 1.0 indicates perfect bilateral symmetry.

GA_signed_pct

Gait Asymmetry (Signed %) - directional asymmetry measure. Positive means right-leg dominant, negative means left-leg dominant.

step_time_cv_pct

Step Time Variability (CV %) - coefficient of variation of step timing. Lower is better.

cycle_time_cv_pct

Gait Cycle Variability (CV %) - coefficient of variation of gait cycle timing. Lower values indicate more rhythmic gait.

total_peaks

Total Peaks - total number of detected foot strikes (both feet combined) during the activity.

total_duration_sec

Total Duration (sec) - total time in seconds to complete the activity from start to finish.

composite_score

Composite Score (0-100) - weighted combination of gait metrics into a single performance score. Higher is better.

composite_score_overall

Overall Composite Score - composite performance score averaged across all four activities.

perf_change_pct_overall

Overall Performance Change (%) - percentage change in overall composite score from baseline.

percentile_rank_absolute

Percentile Rank (Absolute) - patient rank among all patients based on absolute performance (0-100).

Because this is an ongoing research project, the full transformation methodology from raw signal to every derived metric cannot be released yet.

One important disclosure is available: only week 1 data is real. Weeks 2 to 12 are synthetic and were generated for demonstration of longitudinal behavior. The synthetic generation process starts from a random selection of four participants in each performance category (improving, declining, and stable, as recorded in the dataset), then applies an algorithm to generate plausible time-series gait metric values.
